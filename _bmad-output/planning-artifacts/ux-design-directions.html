Direction Evaluation Guide

Compare design directions against ARIA's core principles: transparency, voice primacy, calm confidence, and demo impact.

Evaluation Criteria

  • Layout intuitiveness — which information hierarchy matches ARIA's priorities (browser vs. thinking panel)?
  • Voice state visibility — which direction makes the "always listening" state most evident?
  • Barge-in readiness — which direction makes interruption feel most natural and expected?
  • Trust/confidence signals — which makes step status and confidence scores most readable at a glance?
  • Demo camera-readiness — which looks most impressive in a 4-minute demo video?
  • Novice accessibility — which could Margaret (retired teacher) navigate without confusion?

Facilitator Recommendation
Direction ① (Command Center) is recommended as the primary direction. It maximizes browser real estate while keeping the thinking panel prominent at a fixed 400px — matching the architecture spec exactly. It has the highest demo impact, handles both power users (Ravi) and guided users (Margaret) with the same layout, and the pinned task bar reinforces the always-available interruption model.

Direction ⑥ (Focus Mode) is a strong alternative if you want a cleaner header chrome and tabbed audit log access.

Direction ③ (Immersive) is recommended as a demo-mode variant — maximum visual impact for the submission video, potentially surfaced as a toggle.
